# MBI expense policy

## Where to look

- **Notion** → Finance / People handbook → Expense policy. This is the authoritative source.
- **Ramp** in-app → your card's policy shows the limits and flagged categories Ramp enforces automatically.
- Finance Slack channel `#finance-help` for borderline cases that neither source answers.

## Common policies (day-one knowledge)

Policy specifics shift — read the Notion page at query time for dollar amounts, approvals, and anything dated. Below is the shape of the rules, not the current numbers.

- **Receipts required** for all Ramp-card and reimbursement charges, regardless of amount.
- **Meals**: covered when traveling for work or hosting a client. Internal team meals follow team-budget rules (varies by team). Alcohol allowed only at client dinners (quantity reasonable).
- **Travel**: economy flights, mid-tier hotels, ground transport (Lyft/Uber preferred over rental cars short distances). Long trips may have per-diem.
- **Software**: subscriptions need IT + manager approval. Ramp flags unapproved vendor charges.
- **Gifts**: employee + team gifts go through People, not Ramp (separate process).
- **Personal** charges on Ramp card: not allowed. If accidental, flag immediately and reimburse MBI.

## When to use

- User asks a specific policy question — check Notion first, then fall back to general guidance above.
- User's charge got flagged by Ramp — help them understand the likely policy category and what resolution looks like.
- User is planning a large purchase — direct them to procurement for approval workflow.

## How to act

- Don't quote specific dollar amounts unless you've read them from a live source (Notion, Ramp) this session.
- When a policy is ambiguous, recommend the user flag it with Finance rather than guessing. Offer to open a Jira SD ticket.
- Policy changes are published in Notion + announced in `#company-announcements`. If a user cites an old rule, gently check Notion.
