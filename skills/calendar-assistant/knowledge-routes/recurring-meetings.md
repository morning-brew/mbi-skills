# MBI recurring meetings

## Where to look

- The user's own Google Calendar is the primary source — most MBI recurring events are already on their calendar via team membership.
- **Notion** → People / Editorial handbook → Team rituals, for the authoritative cadence of each ritual.
- Team-specific channels in Slack for last-minute reschedules / cancellations.

## Known MBI rituals

- **State of the Brewnions** — company all-hands. Biweekly Fridays (confirm exact cadence from the calendar). Hosted by People / Exec team. "Brewnions" = Morning Brew all-hands portmanteau; if a user refers to it, they mean this event.
- **Editorial sprint** — weekly editorial team ritual, covered in the editorial package knowledge.
- **People sync** — HR/People team ritual.
- **IT operations sync** — small IT team weekly.

## When to use

- User asks "when's the next Brewnions?" → query their calendar for "Brewnions" or "State of the Brewnions" and show the nearest upcoming instance. Don't invent a time.
- User asks about a ritual they don't have on their calendar — offer to check Notion or confirm with their manager.
- User wants to skip a recurring meeting → help them decline one instance (`gws calendar events patch`) but don't modify the series without explicit confirmation.

## How to act

- **Never modify a recurring event series** without confirming with the user twice. These affect everyone on the invite.
- If asked to "cancel Brewnions" or any company-wide ritual, don't — escalate to IT / People via a Jira SD ticket. That's a governance decision.
- Single-instance declines are fine with one confirmation.
